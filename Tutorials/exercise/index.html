I traveled in Turkey in September 2015. What I found surprised me. I deliberately chose a bus tour as I (wrongly) thought the country was still living in the 19th century meaning it would be difficult to see the country using public transport or a hire car as I had done in the rest of Europe. What I found was that the country, and the people, are no different to the rest of Europe and have advanced considerably into the 21st century. For example, instead of a basic road network, I found 2 or three lane divided highways linking all major towns. Large infrastructure projects were being built everywhere we went. Our road was blocked at a new railway line that “wasn’t there last time” according to our guide. (There was a detour.) Large towns had new hospitals and community housing. The hospitals I believe were free.

Some have praised my response some have despised it. That is the nature of the world. For those equating my article to that of western media bias may I reply that I went to Turkey to see it for myself. I reported above that I was pleasantly surprised with the people and the countries historical tourism.

Whatever makes you think so ill of Türkiye? I have been to that fabled land several times and would return in an instant. The people are welcoming, fascinating, intriguing in their variety. The sights are spectacular, from the architecture to the natural wonders, to the art and handicrafts. Nowhere in the world is there such a magical location as the surreal, swooping rock valleys of Cappadocia. The pure white travertine terraces of Pamukkale ("Cotton Castle" in English) which cascade down the slope look like an out-of-place snowfield amid the green landscape.
